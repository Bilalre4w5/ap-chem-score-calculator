What is a good AP® Chemistry score?

Scores of 3, 4, or 5 are considered passing. A 3 is 'qualified', 4 'well qualified', 5 'extremely well qualified'. Many top colleges require 4+ for credit.

About the AP® Chemistry Exam

The AP Chemistry exam consists of two sections: multiple-choice (60 questions, 90 minutes) and free-response (7 questions, 105 minutes). The multiple-choice section accounts for 50% of your weighted score, and the free-response section accounts for the other 50%. Our calculator uses the most recent scoring guidelines to predict your final AP score (1-5). The curve varies each year based on national performance. Use the dropdown to select different years for a more accurate prediction.

The College Board curves AP exams to maintain consistency across years. Our algorithm uses the official point distributions: each MCQ is worth 1 raw point (max 60). FRQ points: Q1-3 are 10 points each, Q4-7 are 4 points each (total 46 raw FRQ points). Weighted score = (MCQ raw × 0.8333) + (FRQ raw × 1.08696) to normalize to 50/50 weighting. Then the final AP score is determined by cutoffs derived from real exam data.

Use this calculator throughout the year to track your progress. Aim for a weighted score above 70 to be confident for a 4 or 5. Good luck!
